# Content Strategy

## Content Pillars

### 1. Beginner Guides (highest traffic)
- "How to" and "Complete Guide" format
- Target people googling before they buy chickens
- Heavy internal linking to gear reviews
- Examples: "How to raise backyard chickens," "How many chickens should I get"

### 2. Breed Guides (SEO goldmine)
- One article per breed (50+ potential articles)
- Template: origin, temperament, egg production, cold/heat hardiness, pros/cons, best for
- Each links to relevant gear
- Examples: "Rhode Island Red: Complete Breed Guide"

### 3. Gear Reviews & Buyer's Guides (highest conversion)
- "Best X for Y" format
- 5-8 products per roundup, pulled from Amazon
- Research via Amazon reviews, Reddit threads
- Include AffiliateProductCard components
- Examples: "Best Chicken Coops Under $500," "Best Automatic Chicken Doors 2026"

### 4. Care & Health
- Seasonal content (winter prep, summer heat)
- Common problems and solutions
- Feed and nutrition guides

### 5. Cost & Planning
- Budget breakdowns
- Coop plans and dimensions
- Local law guides by state

## Writing Rules
- NO em dashes
- NO AI phrases (delve, landscape, journey, comprehensive, etc.)
- Write like a helpful neighbor who keeps chickens
- Source facts from university extension sites (cite when useful)
- Reddit quotes need attribution and links
- Every article needs 2-3 affiliate product mentions minimum
- FTC disclosure on every page with affiliate links
- 1500-3000 words per article
- 3+ images per article (Wikimedia Commons or Pexels)

## Keyword Research Approach
- Start with Reddit (what questions do beginners ask?)
- Check Google autocomplete and "People Also Ask"
- Target long-tail keywords with buyer intent
- Prioritize keywords where page 1 results are forums, Pinterest, or old WordPress sites
